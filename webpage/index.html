Current Project

We have designed a project of incorporating verdazyl free radicals, which are a class of stable free radicals that can be used for versatile probes for a variety of systems, with crown ethers, well known for its binding properties for sensing applications.

PhD project

An aza crown system coupled with two anthraquinone moieties shows selective fluorescence detection of Mg(II) in the nanomolar range with 2:1 (L:M) binding stoichiometry. In addition, the compound has been used in the bioimaging of Mg (II) in embryonic mouse fibroblast NIH3T3 cells.
